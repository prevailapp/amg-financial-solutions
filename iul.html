Step by Step

How an IUL Actually Works

01

You Pay Your Premium

A portion of your premium covers the cost of insurance. The rest goes into your cash value account — the savings component of your policy.

Think of it like a two-in-one: you're paying for life insurance protection AND building a savings account at the same time. Over time, as your cash value grows, it can even help cover your premium costs.
02

Your Cash Value is Linked to an Index

Your cash value growth is tied to a stock market index like the S&P 500. You participate in market gains without owning stocks directly.

You don't actually invest in the market. Instead, the insurance company uses a formula to credit your account based on how the index performs. This is what gives you growth potential without direct market exposure.
03

The Floor Protects You from Losses

When the market drops, your cash value floor (usually 0%) means your account doesn't lose value. You simply earn 0% that year.

This is one of the most powerful features of an IUL. In 2008, the S&P 500 dropped 38%. IUL policyholders earned 0% — not -38%. That protection can make an enormous difference in long-term wealth accumulation.
04

The Cap Limits Maximum Gains

There's a cap on how much you can earn in a given year — typically 8–16% depending on the carrier. This is the trade-off for floor protection.

If the market returns 25% but your cap is 12%, you earn 12%. If the market returns 8%, you earn 8%. The cap is the price you pay for the floor. For many people, avoiding catastrophic losses is worth giving up some of the upside.
05

Access Your Money Tax-Free

When you're ready to use your cash value — in retirement or otherwise — you access it as a policy loan, which is not taxable income.

This is the retirement strategy component. Unlike a 401k where every dollar you withdraw is taxed as ordinary income, IUL policy loans are tax-free. For high earners, this can save tens of thousands of dollars in taxes over a retirement lifetime.
06

Your Family is Protected for Life

Unlike term insurance that expires, an IUL provides permanent life insurance coverage — your family is protected no matter when you pass away.

And with living benefits included in most modern IUL policies, you can access a portion of your death benefit if you're diagnosed with a critical, chronic, or terminal illness. Your policy works for you while you're alive, not just after you're gone.

Four Reasons People Choose an IUL

📈
Market-Linked Growth

Your cash value grows based on stock index performance. When the market goes up, you benefit. When it goes down, you're protected at zero.

💰
Tax-Free Retirement Income

Access your cash value in retirement as policy loans — completely tax-free, with no IRS contribution limits and no required minimum distributions.

🛡️
Permanent Protection

Unlike term insurance, an IUL covers you for life. Your premiums are locked in, and your family's protection never expires.

❤️
Living Benefits

Access your death benefit early in the event of a critical, chronic, or terminal illness. Your policy works for you while you're still here.
