The Strategy

What is an IUL?

An Indexed Universal Life (IUL) policy is a permanent life insurance product that builds cash value over time — linked to a stock market index like the S&P 500, but protected from market losses.

Think of it as a financial tool that grows when the market goes up, stays flat when it goes down, and provides a tax-free income stream in retirement.

For high earners who've maxed out their 401(k), or anyone looking for an alternative to traditional retirement accounts, an IUL can be a powerful addition to your financial plan.

📈

Market-Linked Growth

Cash value grows based on stock index performance — you participate in the upside without direct market exposure.

🛡️

Protected from Loss

A floor (often 0%) means your cash value never decreases due to market downturns.

💰

Tax-Free Retirement Income

Withdraw from your cash value in retirement as loans — tax-free, with no IRS contribution limits.

🏠

Living Benefits

Access your death benefit early in cases of critical, chronic, or terminal illness.

Is This Right for You?

An IUL Works Best For You If…

01
You're a High Earner

You earn $75,000+ and have maxed out your 401(k) or Roth IRA. You're looking for additional tax-advantaged growth with no IRS contribution limits.

02
You're Self-Employed

You're a business owner or freelancer without employer-sponsored retirement plans. An IUL gives you flexible, tax-efficient wealth building on your own terms.

03
You Want Tax-Free Retirement

You're concerned about future tax rates and want a source of retirement income that won't be taxed — regardless of what happens to tax laws.

04
You're a Parent

You want permanent life insurance coverage that also builds cash value your children or grandchildren can benefit from for decades to come.

05
You're Risk-Averse

You want market-linked growth but can't stomach watching your retirement savings drop 30% in a bad year. The floor protects you from losses.

06
You Want Living Benefits

You want a policy that works while you're alive — not just at death. Access your benefit early in the event of a serious illness or health crisis.
